Name:

Granny Square

Type:

Pattern

Creator:

Joony

Source of Image: Joony

Description:

A granny square is a staple of any quilt, pillows, or other long crochet projects. Here's how you can make one of your own!


Steps:

1. First, you would want to make the center ring. To do so, chain 4, then insert your hook into the first chain stitch.
2. Then, make a slip stitch to connect the chain stitches into a circle.
3. Here is where you should start the first round of stiches. The steps will be listed as 3a-3e:

3 a. Chain 3 stitches. These will count as a double crochet
3 b. Work in 2 double crochets into the center ring so that you have 3 double crochets in total. This is the first granny cluster of the row. Chain 3 stitches.
3 c. Make the second granny cluster by working it into the center. Chain 3 stitches.
3 d. Make the third and fourth granny clusters by repeating the steps above. Make sure to chain 3 stitches after each cluster.
3 e. Join the round together by slip stitching into the top of the first chain 3 section.

4. The second round follows as such:
4 a. Chain 4 stitches, this counts as the first double crochet of this round.
4 b. For the corner, you would do 3 double crochets, chain 3 stitches, 3 double crochets, then chain 1 stitch.
4 c. Repeat the previous step 2 more times.
4 d. The final corner will go as such: 3 double chains, chain 3 stitches, 2 double crochets.
4 e. Join the round together with a slip stitch to the third chain of the first chain stitches.

5. The third round goes as such:
5 a. Make the first double crochet by chaining 3 stitches.
5 b. Within the chain 1 space below, work in 2 double crochets and chain 1 stitch. This is the first granny cluster of this round.
5 c. In the next chain 3 corner, do 3 double crochets, chain 3 stitches, 3 double crochets, chain 1 stitch.
5 d. Do 3 double crochets and chain 1 into the next chain 1 space below.
5 e. Repeat the previous 2 steps until you reach the beginning of the round.
5 f. Join it together by slip stitching into the starting chain 3 stitch.

6. The fourth round proceeds as such:
6 a. Chain 4 stitches. This is the first double crochet and first chain 1 stitch of this round.
6 b. Into the chain 1 stitch from round 3, work in 3 double chains and chain 1 stitch.
6 c. Into the next chain 3 stitch, work in 3 double crochets, chain 3 stitches, 3 double crochets, and chain 1 stitch.
6 d. Repeat the previous 2 steps for the remain chain 1 and chain 3 stitches.
6 e. In the last chain 1 stitch, work in 2 double crochets.
6 f. Join it together with a slip stitch into the top of the first chain 3 stitch.

You can create larger squares by repeating the third and fourth rounds as necessary.
